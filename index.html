The ocean is a continuous body of salt water covering approximately 70.8% of Earth, containing 97% of its water and regulating climate, carbon, and oxygen production. It is divided into five main basins—Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Southern, and Arctic—and holds most of the planet's biodiversity.

More to know

The Pacific Ocean is the world's largest and deepest ocean, covering approximately 165.25 million square kilometers ( million square miles), which is about 46% of Earth's water surface and 32% of its total surface area.





The sea, covering over 70% of Earth’s surface and containing 97% of its water, is the planet's largest habitat and primary climate regulator.

Vital Climate Regulator: The ocean absorbs 90% of excess heat from human activity, regulating global temperatures.

Immense Biodiversity: Home to over 240,000 known species, with potentially millions more yet to be discovered in the deep sea.
